pregnant women who suffer extreme morning sickness are at greater risk of their children having language and speech delays , warn researchers .
about 10,000 women a year in the uk are affected by the debilitating hyperemesis gravidarum ( hg ) - a severe form of morning sickness which causes constant vomiting , crippling weakness and dehydration .
many are denied vital yet relatively cheap treatment by gps who expect them to simply ‘ get on with it’ or end their pregnancy .
