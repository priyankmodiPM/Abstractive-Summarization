two women who were part of a group that repeatedly searched mississippi 's capital city for black people to assault were sentenced thursday to multiple years in federal prison for their role in the 2011 hate killing of 47-year-old james craig anderson . u.s. district judge henry t. wingate on thursday sentenced shelbie brooke richards , 21 , of pearl to eight years in prison on one count each of conspiracy to commit a hate crime and concealing the crime by lying to police . in a separate hearing earlier thursday , wingate also sentenced sarah adelia graves , 22 , of crystal springs to five years in prison for one count of conspiracy to commit a hate crime . went in search of black men to assault : shelbie brooke richards , 21 ( left ) was sentenced to 8 years in prison and sarah adelia graves , 22 , ( left ) was given 5 years for their role in the horrific 2011 murder of 47-year-old jackson , mississippi man james craig anderson . horrific : graves and richards were in the car when a group of white teens drove from their suburban town after a party with the specific intent of assaulting black men in jackson , which they called ' jafrica ' . james anderson was one of their victims . he died after being beaten and run over by a truck . both women were riding in a truck that fatally ran over anderson in june 2011 . anderson died after being beaten and run over . both women read letters of apology to anderson 's bereaved family , writes the clarion-ledger .
